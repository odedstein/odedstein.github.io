An Exploration of Knots and Links for Physical Simulation in Computer Graphics

Oded Stein        Eitan Grinspun

Columbia University       University of Toronto

Abstract

We examine the application of algebraic knot theory to the physical simulation of curves in space in the context of computer graphics and physical simulation. In the first part, the classical linking number of Gauss is generalized to open curves, and a method is presented to efficiently compute these linking numbers as well as optimize them (based on the work of Qinami and Grinspun [2017]). In the second part, we present a method to compute the Vassiliev invariants of closed curves in R3, and explore a strategy to manipulate closed curves in space to achieve desired knot invariants, as well as use Vassiliev invariants for contact simulation. Preliminary results of applications to curves in R3 are shown.
